# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

SAIS is built on a single foundational belief: the people who grow food, manage land, and steward natural resources deserve tools that work for them — not against them. That belief extends to how we build this project and how we treat each other in this community.

We are committed to maintaining a welcoming, respectful, and productive environment for everyone who contributes, regardless of experience level, background, or perspective.

## Expected Behavior

- Communicate with respect and good faith. Assume positive intent.
- Provide constructive, specific feedback. Critique ideas, not people.
- Acknowledge the expertise and contributions of others.
- Accept that reasonable people can disagree on technical and design decisions.
- Prioritize the mission: building infrastructure that serves operators, not corporations.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks of any kind.
- Discriminatory language or behavior based on any personal characteristic.
- Deliberate introduction of vulnerabilities, backdoors, or telemetry that violates the sovereignty principles of the project.
- Misrepresenting the project's capabilities or licensing terms to potential users or partners.
- Using the project's community spaces to promote proprietary, extractive alternatives.

## Enforcement

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in removal from the project's community spaces and, in serious cases, a permanent ban from contributing. Reports can be submitted via GitHub's reporting tools or by contacting the project maintainers directly.

## The Bigger Picture

This project exists because the incumbent model extracts value from the people doing the hardest work. The community building SAIS should reflect the opposite: a collaborative, transparent, and equitable environment where the work benefits everyone who contributes to it.
